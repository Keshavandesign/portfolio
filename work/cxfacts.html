B2B SaaS Survey Tool 30 Days Product Design

CXFacts
Custom Question

CXFacts had brilliant questions but they were fixed. This is what happened when we gave users the pen, and why two Nordic banks took notice.

Scroll
The Problem
CXFacts sits between banks and businesses it runs surveys, surfaces insights, drives action. The quality bar was intentionally high: only vetted questions, nothing sloppy. But that same rigour became a bottleneck. Users knew exactly what they needed to ask. The platform just wouldn't let them.
The Solution
A native question builder multiple-choice, rating scale, open text built directly into the platform users already trusted. Quality stays high. Control shifts to the user.

Wireframes &
Design System

Wireframes first deliberately rough. I wanted the founders reacting to structure, not colour. Once the interaction model was signed off, I stepped into the existing design system and extended it: three new icons for question types, a bar chart for scale questions, a donut for multiple choice. The system had strong bones. It mostly just needed new components, not a redesign.

Handoff &
Developer Collaboration

Handoff wasn't just exporting assets it was writing for the developers. Every component had a note: hover states, error handling, edge cases. We split Jira by platform (Bank and Business) and used child issues so nothing got buried in a long ticket. The goal was zero back-and-forth during implementation. Mostly we hit it.

Challenges & Iterations

Where the design
got better.

⚙ Iteration 01

Previously Used Questions

First round of wireframe testing surfaced something I hadn't designed for: users didn't want to rebuild the same question twice. "I asked this last quarter why can't I just reuse it?" That became a side drawer, reachable right from the question creation screen tucked away until needed. The kind of thing that feels obvious in hindsight.

⚙ Iteration 02

Character Counter

Nobody writes a 400-character survey question. Except when they do. During testing, a user hammered the question field and the layout collapsed. We benchmarked character limits across Google Forms and SurveyMonkey, landed on counts that covered real usage, then added a live counter. Not glamorous. Completely necessary.

01

Getting a developer into ideation not just handoff changed the quality of decisions. They flagged three interaction patterns I'd designed that were technically expensive for zero UX benefit. Earlier involvement means fewer late surprises, for everyone.

02

Writing dev notes is design work. The better I explained my intent not just what it looked like but why the fewer revision cycles we needed. On this project: zero major revisions post-handoff. I'll take that.

03

Before designing a single screen, I spent two days inside Google Forms, Microsoft Forms, and SurveyMonkey not to copy, but to understand what people already know how to do. Muscle memory is free usability. Build with it.
